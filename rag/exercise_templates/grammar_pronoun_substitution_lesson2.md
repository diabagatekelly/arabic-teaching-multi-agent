# Pronoun Substitution Practice Template

## Pattern
Change sentences by substituting pronouns and adjusting agreement.

## Example 1: Change Subject
**Original:** أَنَا طَالِب (ana taalib - I am a student, male)
**Change to هُوَ:** هُوَ طَالِب (huwa taalib - He is a student)
**Change to هِيَ:** هِيَ طَالِبَة (hiya taaliba - She is a student)
**Note:** Gender of predicate noun changes with هِيَ

## Example 2: Gender Switch
**Original:** هُوَ مُدَرِّس (huwa mudarris - He is a teacher)
**Change to feminine:** هِيَ مُدَرِّسَة (hiya mudarrisa - She is a teacher)
**Changes:** Both pronoun AND noun form change

## Example 3: From 1st to 2nd Person
**Original:** أَنَا طَالِب جَدِيد (ana taalib jadiid - I am a new student)
**Change to أَنْتَ:** أَنْتَ طَالِب جَدِيد (anta taalib jadiid - You are a new student)
**Note:** Rest of sentence stays same for masculine

**Change to أَنْتِ (feminine):** أَنْتِ طَالِبَة جَدِيدَة (anti taaliba jadiida)
**Note:** Noun and adjective both change for feminine

## Example 4: Complex Substitution
**Original:** هِيَ مُدَرِّسَة في المَدْرَسَة الكَبِيرَة
(hiya mudarrisa fi al-madrasa al-kabiira - She is a teacher at the big school)
**Change to masculine:** هُوَ مُدَرِّس في المَدْرَسَة الكَبِيرَة
**Note:** Only subject and predicate change, rest stays same

## Generation Guidelines
- Start with simple pronoun swaps
- Progress to gender-sensitive substitutions
- Show cascading agreement changes
- Include 1st, 2nd, and 3rd person
- Require both pronoun and noun/adjective changes
- Use Lesson 2 patterns
