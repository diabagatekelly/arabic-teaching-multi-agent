# Arabic Teaching LLM - Curriculum Sequence

## MVP Curriculum (8 Core Lessons)

This sequence focuses on foundational grammar that enables basic communication. Topics are ordered by prerequisite relationships and pedagogical logic.

---

### **Lesson 1: Gender & The Definite Article**

**Grammar Focus:**
- Masculine vs feminine nouns
- Definite article (ال - al-)
- Recognizing gender markers (ة - taa marbuta)

**Why First:**
- Gender affects ALL Arabic grammar (adjectives, verbs, pronouns)
- Definite article is used constantly
- Foundation for everything else

**Vocabulary (10 words):**
- Mix of masculine and feminine nouns from everyday categories
- Example: كِتَاب (book-M), طَاوِلَة (table-F), بَيْت (house-M), مَدْرَسَة (school-F)

**Sample Exercises:**
- Identify gender of nouns
- Add definite article correctly (ال + sun/moon letters)
- Distinguish between الكِتَاب and كِتَاب

**Prerequisites:** None (starting point)

---

### **Lesson 2: Subject Pronouns & Nominal Sentences**

**Grammar Focus:**
- Subject pronouns: أَنَا، أَنْتَ، أَنْتِ، هُوَ، هِيَ، نَحْنُ، أَنْتُمْ، هُمْ
- Nominal sentences (جُمْلَة اسْمِيَّة) - no verb needed
- Structure: Subject + Predicate (هُوَ طَالِب - He is a student)

**Why Second:**
- Simplest sentence type (no verb conjugation yet)
- Introduces pronouns needed for all future tenses
- Immediate practical use: "I am...", "This is..."

**Vocabulary (10 words):**
- 4 words from Lesson 1 (review)
- 6 new words: occupations/descriptions (طَالِب student, مُعَلِّم teacher, كَبِير big, صَغِير small)

**Sample Exercises:**
- Form sentences: "أَنَا طَالِب" (I am a student)
- Match pronouns to people
- Identify subject and predicate

**Prerequisites:** Lesson 1 (need to know gender and definite article)

---

### **Lesson 3: Noun-Adjective Phrases & Agreement**

**Grammar Focus:**
- Adjectives follow nouns
- Adjectives must agree in: gender, definiteness, number
- Examples: كِتَابٌ كَبِيرٌ (a big book), الكِتَابُ الكَبِيرُ (the big book)

**Why Third:**
- Builds on gender knowledge from Lesson 1
- Uses nominal sentence structure from Lesson 2
- Essential for descriptive language

**Vocabulary (10 words):**
- 5 words from Lessons 1-2 (selected by spaced repetition)
- 5 new adjectives: كَبِير، صَغِير، جَمِيل، جَدِيد، قَدِيم، طَوِيل، قَصِير

**Sample Exercises:**
- Match adjectives to nouns with correct agreement
- Convert indefinite → definite phrases (كِتَابٌ كَبِيرٌ → الكِتَابُ الكَبِيرُ)
- Create descriptions of objects

**Prerequisites:** Lessons 1 & 2 (gender, definiteness, nominal sentences)

---

### **Lesson 4: Present Tense Verbs**

**Grammar Focus:**
- Present tense conjugation (الْمُضَارِع)
- Verb prefixes for each pronoun: أَ، تَ، يَ، نَ
- Basic verb patterns (Form I)

**Why Fourth:**
- First introduction to verbs
- Enables action sentences beyond "X is Y"
- Foundation for past/future tenses

**Vocabulary (10 words):**
- 4 words from previous lessons (nouns/adjectives for use in sentences)
- 6 new verbs: يَكْتُبُ، يَقْرَأُ، يَأْكُلُ، يَشْرَبُ، يَذْهَبُ، يَجِيءُ

**Sample Exercises:**
- Conjugate verbs for different pronouns
- Translate: "I write", "He eats", "She goes"
- Form sentences: أَكْتُبُ الكِتَابَ (I write the book)

**Prerequisites:** Lesson 2 (must know pronouns)

---

### **Lesson 5: Past Tense Verbs**

**Grammar Focus:**
- Past tense conjugation (الْمَاضِي)
- Suffix-based conjugation (not prefix like present)
- Feminine marker: ـَتْ

**Why Fifth:**
- Natural progression from present tense
- Different conjugation pattern (reinforces understanding)
- Enables talking about completed actions

**Vocabulary (10 words):**
- 5 words from Lessons 1-4 (selected by spaced repetition)
- 5 new past tense verbs: كَتَبَ، قَرَأَ، أَكَلَ، شَرِبَ، ذَهَبَ

**Sample Exercises:**
- Convert present → past tense
- Practice feminine past tense (كَتَبَتْ)
- Translate: "She wrote", "They ate", "We went"

**Prerequisites:** Lessons 2 & 4 (pronouns and present tense)

---

### **Lesson 6: Negation**

**Grammar Focus:**
- Negating present tense: لَا + present verb (لَا أَكْتُبُ - I don't write)
- Negating past tense: مَا + past verb (مَا كَتَبْتُ - I didn't write)
- Negating nominal sentences: لَيْسَ (Lesson 8)

**Why Sixth:**
- Essential for practical communication
- Uses verbs from Lessons 4 & 5
- Simple pattern to learn

**Vocabulary (10 words):**
- 8 words from previous lessons (verbs and nouns for use in negative sentences)
- 2 new vocabulary items related to negation context

**Sample Exercises:**
- Convert positive → negative sentences
- Translate: "I don't eat", "He didn't go", "We don't read"
- Error correction: identify incorrect negation

**Prerequisites:** Lessons 4 & 5 (must know present and past tense)

---

### **Lesson 7: Asking Questions (Yes/No & Question Words)**

**Grammar Focus:**
- Yes/no questions: هَلْ + statement (هَلْ تَكْتُبُ؟ - Do you write?)
- Question words: مَا، مَنْ، أَيْنَ، مَتَى، كَيْفَ، لِمَاذَا
- Word order in questions

**Why Seventh:**
- Builds on all previous sentence types
- Enables two-way conversation
- Practical communication skill

**Vocabulary (10 words):**
- 7 words from previous lessons (spaced repetition)
- 3 new words for question contexts (places, times)

**Sample Exercises:**
- Form yes/no questions from statements
- Match question words to appropriate contexts
- Answer questions with complete sentences

**Prerequisites:** Lessons 1-6 (uses all sentence types learned so far)

---

### **Lesson 8: Possessive Pronouns & Idaafah (إِضَافَة)**

**Grammar Focus:**
- Possessive pronoun suffixes: ـِي، ـَكَ، ـَكِ، ـُهُ، ـَهَا
- Idaafah (construct state): linking two nouns without preposition
- Example: كِتَابُ الطَّالِبِ (the student's book), كِتَابِي (my book)

**Why Eighth:**
- Natural after learning basic sentences
- Enables expressing ownership and relationships
- Two methods: pronouns vs idaafah

**Vocabulary (10 words):**
- 6 words from previous lessons (nouns that can be possessed)
- 4 new family/relationship words: أَبٌ، أُمٌّ، أَخٌ، أُخْتٌ

**Sample Exercises:**
- Add possessive pronouns to nouns: كِتَاب → كِتَابِي
- Form idaafah phrases: "the teacher's book" = كِتَابُ المُعَلِّمِ
- Translate: "my house", "her school", "his brother"

**Prerequisites:** Lessons 1 & 2 (definiteness and noun structure)

- **Lesson 19:** Direct vs indirect questions format
- **Lesson 20:** Fronted predicate (emphasis)

- **Lesson 22:** لَيْسَ (negation verb "is not")

- **Lesson 24:** Telling time

## Vocabulary Pairing Strategy

Each lesson uses 10 words total: mix of new words + review words (selected by spaced repetition).

### Suggested Vocabulary Themes by Lesson

|--------|---------|---------------|
| 1 | Everyday objects | كِتَاب، طَاوِلَة، بَيْت، مَدْرَسَة، قَلَم |
| 2 | People & descriptions | طَالِب، مُعَلِّم، وَلَد، بِنْت، إِنْسَان |

| 5 | Past tense verbs | كَتَبَ، قَرَأَ، أَكَلَ، شَرِبَ، ذَهَبَ |
| 6 | Mixed (negation contexts) | Review verbs + new nouns |
| 7 | Places & time words | مَكَان، زَمَان، الْيَوْمَ، غَدًا، هُنَا |
| 8 | Family & relationships | أَبٌ، أُمٌّ، أَخٌ، أُخْتٌ، صَدِيق |

**Note:** Themes are suggestive. User can select alternative themes, or use default pairing.

## Progression Gates

Student must meet these criteria to unlock next lesson:

✅ **Completion**: All exercises in current lesson completed
✅ **Accuracy**: ≥80% correct on lesson exercises
✅ **Word Mastery**: All new words from current lesson reach at least 3-day spaced repetition interval

**Flexible option**: App can require "any 2 of 3" instead of all three criteria.

---

## Design Notes

1. **Lesson 1-2** establish foundations without verb conjugation (easier entry point)
2. **Lesson 3** adds complexity (agreement rules) but stays within nominal sentences
3. **Lesson 4-5** introduce verbs (present then past)
4. **Lesson 6-7** add essential modifiers (negation, questions)
5. **Lesson 8** enables possession/relationships

This sequence allows students to form increasingly complex sentences while maintaining clear prerequisite chains.
